# KitchenAid appliance care and service notes

Original brand-oriented guidance (not sourced from any manufacturer manual).
Nothing here overrides the safety interrupt or the deterministic troubleshooting
trees in `app/knowledge/*.yaml`.

## About the brand at Sears

KitchenAid (part of the Whirlpool family) is a kitchen-focused, upper-tier
brand that Sears sells and services: dishwashers, refrigerators, ranges, wall
ovens, cooktops, and microwaves. Because of the Whirlpool relationship,
KitchenAid units often share parts families with Whirlpool and Kenmore models.

## Service notes

- KitchenAid fault codes generally follow the Whirlpool-style "F"/"E"
  convention; ask the caller to read the code exactly as displayed, in order,
  before resetting the unit.
- The rating plate is typically on the inner door edge or door frame of
  dishwashers and wall ovens, and on an interior side wall of refrigerators.
- KitchenAid dishwashers commonly sit at the quieter end of the range —
  like Bosch, a "not running" report is sometimes a machine quietly
  mid-cycle; have the caller check the status lights first.

## Care notes

- Clean the dishwasher filter monthly and keep spray-arm nozzles clear.
- On built-in refrigerators, condenser airflow paths are tighter than on
  freestanding units — keep grilles dust-free and vacuum coils about twice a
  year.
- On ranges and wall ovens, note any error code exactly as displayed and
  record the model number from the frame plate before calling; door-lock and
  sensor codes look alike but are different repairs.
