# Samsung appliance care and service notes

Original brand-oriented guidance (not sourced from any manufacturer manual).
Nothing here overrides the safety interrupt or the deterministic troubleshooting
trees in `app/knowledge/*.yaml`.

## About the brand at Sears

Samsung is a major appliance brand that Sears sells and services: washers,
dryers, refrigerators (including French-door and side-by-side units with
through-the-door ice and water), dishwashers, ranges, and microwaves. Samsung
units tend to be display-heavy, which makes error codes especially useful on a
service call.

## Service notes

- Samsung error codes are usually short letter/digit combinations shown on the
  front display. Ask the caller to read the code exactly as displayed — a
  letter-for-letter reading matters, because visually similar codes point at
  different subsystems.
- Many Samsung units chime or flash rather than hold the code; if the code has
  disappeared, the caller can often surface it again by reproducing the failed
  cycle for a minute or two.
- The model and serial plate is typically inside the door frame on laundry and
  dishwashers, and on an interior side wall on refrigerators.

## Care notes

- Samsung French-door refrigerators are sensitive to blocked interior vents —
  advise callers to keep food away from the rear vent openings so air can
  circulate between compartments.
- On front-load washers, run the machine's self-clean cycle periodically and
  wipe the door gasket dry to keep odor down.
- Ice maker complaints should start with the basics: water supply on, bin
  seated correctly, and the freezer compartment actually at temperature.
