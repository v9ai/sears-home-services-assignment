# Whirlpool appliance care and service notes

Original brand-oriented guidance (not sourced from any manufacturer manual).
Nothing here overrides the safety interrupt or the deterministic troubleshooting
trees in `app/knowledge/*.yaml`.

## About the brand at Sears

Whirlpool is one of the largest appliance manufacturers in North America and a
long-standing Sears partner — many Kenmore-badged laundry machines were built by
Whirlpool. Sears sells and services Whirlpool washers, dryers, refrigerators,
freezers, dishwashers, ranges, ovens, cooktops, and microwaves.

## Service notes

- Whirlpool front-load washers and matching dryers share a parts family with
  several Kenmore and Maytag models, which usually shortens parts sourcing.
- Fault codes on Whirlpool units are typically an "F" code, often paired with
  an "E" code (for example, F-then-a-digit followed by E-then-a-digit). Ask the
  caller to read the code exactly as displayed, in order, before power-cycling —
  clearing the display can make the visit slower, not faster.
- The model and serial plate is commonly inside the door frame on laundry and
  dishwashers, behind the bottom drawer or on an interior wall on
  refrigerators, and under the lid or behind the storage drawer on ranges.

## Care notes

- Wipe front-load washer door gaskets dry after heavy use and leave the door
  ajar between loads to discourage odor.
- Vacuum refrigerator condenser coils about twice a year, and keep the dryer's
  exterior vent duct clear — the same general longevity habits in the
  maintenance tips guide apply to every Whirlpool category Sears services.
