# Amana appliance care and service notes

Original brand-oriented guidance (not sourced from any manufacturer manual).
Nothing here overrides the safety interrupt or the deterministic troubleshooting
trees in `app/knowledge/*.yaml`.

## About the brand at Sears

Amana (part of the Whirlpool family) is a value-oriented brand that Sears sells
and services: washers, dryers, refrigerators, dishwashers, ranges, microwaves,
and HVAC equipment. Amana units are mechanically straightforward and share many
parts families with Whirlpool-built machines.

## Service notes

- Simpler Amana models may signal faults with blinking-light patterns rather
  than a text display; ask the caller to describe exactly which lights blink
  and in what rhythm — it's the value-line equivalent of an error code.
- On models that do have displays, fault codes follow the Whirlpool-style
  "F"/"E" convention; read them exactly as displayed before power-cycling.
- The rating plate is usually inside the door frame on laundry and
  dishwashers, on an interior side wall on refrigerators, and behind the
  drawer on ranges.

## Care notes

- Value-line top-load washers still need level feet — walking or banging in
  spin is usually leveling or load balance, not a failure.
- Clean the dryer lint screen every load and the exterior vent yearly.
- For Amana HVAC units, replace or wash filters on schedule and keep the
  outdoor unit clear of debris; reduced airflow is the most common
  "not cooling enough" cause and maps to the HVAC troubleshooting tree.
