# GE appliance care and service notes

Original brand-oriented guidance (not sourced from any manufacturer manual).
Nothing here overrides the safety interrupt or the deterministic troubleshooting
trees in `app/knowledge/*.yaml`.

## About the brand at Sears

GE (General Electric, today GE Appliances) is a major full-line brand that
Sears sells and services: refrigerators, freezers, dishwashers, ranges, wall
ovens, cooktops, microwaves, washers, dryers, and room air conditioners. The
GE Profile and Café lines are the brand's higher-end tiers and are serviced the
same way.

## Service notes

- GE model numbers usually start with letters that encode the product line;
  ask callers to read the full model string from the rating plate rather than
  the marketing name on the door.
- The rating plate is commonly on the interior side wall of refrigerators,
  inside the door frame or along the tub rim on dishwashers, and behind the
  door or under the backsplash on laundry units.
- Newer GE units may show two-character or spelled-out error codes on the
  display; have the caller note the code exactly as displayed before resetting
  anything.

## Care notes

- GE refrigerators with bottom-front condenser coils benefit from a
  twice-yearly coil vacuuming — reduced cooling with a constantly running
  compressor is very often just dusty coils.
- On GE electric ranges, keep drip areas clean and dry; on gas models, any
  gas smell is an immediate safety escalation, never a troubleshooting step.
