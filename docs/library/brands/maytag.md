# Maytag appliance care and service notes

Original brand-oriented guidance (not sourced from any manufacturer manual).
Nothing here overrides the safety interrupt or the deterministic troubleshooting
trees in `app/knowledge/*.yaml`.

## About the brand at Sears

Maytag is a laundry-first brand (now part of the Whirlpool family) that Sears
sells and services: washers, dryers, refrigerators, dishwashers, and ranges.
Because of the Whirlpool relationship, many Maytag units share parts families
with Whirlpool and Kenmore models, which usually helps parts availability.

## Service notes

- Maytag fault codes follow the Whirlpool-style "F"/"E" convention on many
  models; ask the caller to read the code exactly as displayed, in order,
  before power-cycling the unit.
- The model and serial plate is commonly inside the door frame on laundry and
  dishwashers, and on an interior wall on refrigerators.
- Maytag's reputation is durability-focused top-load laundry; on older
  top-loaders, a machine that agitates but won't spin is one of the most
  common calls and maps directly to the washer troubleshooting tree.

## Care notes

- Keep washer lids or doors ajar between loads and wipe gaskets dry.
- Clean the dryer lint screen every load and the exterior vent duct at least
  yearly — long dry times on a Maytag dryer are far more often airflow than
  heater failure.
- Level the machine feet if a washer has started walking or banging in spin;
  an out-of-level machine wears components faster.
