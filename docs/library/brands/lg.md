# LG appliance care and service notes

Original brand-oriented guidance (not sourced from any manufacturer manual).
Nothing here overrides the safety interrupt or the deterministic troubleshooting
trees in `app/knowledge/*.yaml`.

## About the brand at Sears

LG is a major appliance brand that Sears sells and services: front-load and
top-load washers, dryers, refrigerators, dishwashers, ranges, and microwaves.
LG also supplied some Kenmore Elite models, so an LG parts family can turn up
behind a Kenmore badge.

## Service notes

- LG units display two-letter or letter-digit error codes; ask the caller to
  read the code exactly as displayed before unplugging or resetting the unit.
- Many LG washers and refrigerators use an inverter (variable-speed)
  compressor or direct-drive motor — a unit that sounds different from an
  older machine is not necessarily faulty, but new grinding, clicking, or
  buzzing that wasn't there before is worth a service visit.
- The rating plate is usually inside the door frame on laundry and
  dishwashers, and on an interior side wall on refrigerators.

## Care notes

- On LG front-load washers, run the tub-clean cycle periodically, wipe the
  door gasket dry, and leave the door ajar between loads.
- Keep refrigerator condenser areas clear and vacuum the coils about twice a
  year, per the general maintenance tips guide.
- For dryers, the same rule as every brand applies: a clear exterior vent duct
  is the cheapest fix for long dry times.
