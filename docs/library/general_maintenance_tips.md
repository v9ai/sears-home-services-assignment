## Dryer vent maintenance

Clean the dryer's exterior vent duct at least once a year, and check it sooner if
drying times have crept up or the outside vent flap barely moves with the dryer
running. A partially blocked vent makes the dryer work harder and run hotter than
it should — routine annual cleaning is cheap insurance against exactly the kind of
airflow problems that eventually show up as "not heating enough" or, in the worst
case, overheating. If the vent run is long, has several bends, or you can't
inspect it yourself, a professional duct cleaning is worth scheduling periodically
rather than guessing.

## Refrigerator coil care

Vacuum the condenser coils (behind the bottom grille on most models, or on the
back on older ones) roughly twice a year. Dust-caked coils make the compressor run
longer to hit the same temperature, which shows up as higher energy use now and
faster wear over time.

## General longevity habits

- Keep a few inches of clearance around any appliance that vents heat (fridge,
  dryer, oven) so airflow isn't restricted.
- Wipe door gaskets clean on washers, dryers, dishwashers, and refrigerators —
  trapped debris is the single most common cause of a seal that stops sealing.
- Note any error code exactly as displayed before calling for help; it's usually
  the fastest way to point a technician at the right part.
